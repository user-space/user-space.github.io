Privacy Policy

Website Visitors

Like most website operators, UserSpace Inc. collects non-personally-identifying information of the sort that web browsers and servers typically make available, such as the browser type, language preference, referring site, and the date and time of each visitor request. UserSpace Inc.'s purpose in collecting non-personally identifying information is to better understand how UserSpace Inc.'s visitors use its website. From time to time, UserSpace Inc. may release non-personally-identifying information in the aggregate, e.g., by publishing a report on trends in the usage of its website.

UserSpace Inc. also collects potentially personally-identifying information like Internet Protocol (IP) addresses. UserSpace Inc. does not use such information to identify its visitors, however, and does not disclose such information, other than under the same circumstances that it uses and discloses personally-identifying information, as described below.

Gathering of Personally-Identifying Information

Certain visitors to UserSpace Inc.'s websites choose to interact with UserSpace Inc. in ways that require UserSpace Inc. to gather personally-identifying information. The amount and type of information that UserSpace Inc. gathers depends on the nature of the interaction. For example, we ask visitors who use our forums to provide a username and email address. In each case, UserSpace Inc. collects such information only insofar as is necessary or appropriate to fulfill the purpose of the visitor's interaction with UserSpace Inc.. UserSpace Inc. does not disclose personally-identifying information other than as described below. And visitors can always refuse to supply personally-identifying information, with the caveat that it may prevent them from engaging in certain website-related activities.

Aggregated Statistics

UserSpace Inc. may collect statistics about the behavior of visitors to its websites. For instance, UserSpace Inc. may reveal how many downloads a particular version got. However, UserSpace Inc. does not disclose personally-identifying information other than as described below.

Protection of Certain Personally-Identifying Information

UserSpace Inc. discloses potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information only to those of its employees, contractors, and affiliated organizations that (i) need to know that information in order to process it on UserSpace Inc.'s behalf or to provide services available at UserSpace Inc.'s websites, and (ii) that have agreed not to disclose it to others. Some of those employees, contractors and affiliated organizations may be located outside of your home country; by using UserSpace Inc.'s websites, you consent to the transfer of such information to them. UserSpace Inc. will not rent or sell potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information to anyone. Other than to its employees, contractors, and affiliated organizations, as described above, UserSpace Inc. discloses potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information only when required to do so by law, or when UserSpace Inc. believes in good faith that disclosure is reasonably necessary to protect the property or rights of UserSpace Inc., third parties, or the public at large. If you are a registered user of a UserSpace Inc. website and have supplied your email address, UserSpace Inc. may occasionally send you an email to tell you about new features, solicit your feedback, or just keep you up to date with what's going on with UserSpace Inc. and our products. We primarily use our blog to communicate this type of information, so we expect to keep this type of email to a minimum. If you send us a request (for example via a support email or via one of our feedback mechanisms), we reserve the right to publish it in order to help us clarify or respond to your request or to help us support other users. UserSpace Inc. takes all measures reasonably necessary to protect against the unauthorized access, use, alteration, or destruction of potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information.

Collecting Enterprise Edition usage data

UserSpace Inc. collects information about usage from each UserSpace instance (Community Edition and Enterprise Edition) through a usage ping. The usage ping sends a payload containing data such as total number of projects and pipelines, as well as license information and hostname to UserSpace Inc. Only aggregates of usage data is sent: no project names or other content is sent to UserSpace Inc. You can view the exact payload of the usage ping in the administration panel in UserSpace. Here one can also opt-out of the usage ping. You can read more about the usage ping in the documentation.

Cookies

A cookie is a string of information that a website stores on a visitor's computer, and that the visitor's browser provides to the website each time the visitor returns. UserSpace Inc. uses cookies to help UserSpace Inc. identify and track visitors, their usage of UserSpace Inc. website, and their website access preferences. UserSpace Inc. visitors who do not wish to have cookies placed on their computers should set their browsers to refuse cookies before using UserSpace Inc.'s websites, with the drawback that certain features of UserSpace Inc.'s websites may not function properly without the aid of cookies.

The use of cookies by third parties is not covered by our privacy policy. For example, when we include content from other sites (e.g., Google Analytics, Marketo, etc.) these sites may also set their own cookies and we do not have access or control over these cookies. See Google Analytics and Marketo privacy policies for more information.

Privacy Policy Changes

Although most changes are likely to be minor, UserSpace Inc. may change its Privacy Policy from time to time, and in UserSpace Inc.'s sole discretion. UserSpace Inc. encourages visitors to frequently check this page for any changes to its Privacy Policy. Your continued use of this site after any change in this Privacy Policy will constitute your acceptance of such change.
